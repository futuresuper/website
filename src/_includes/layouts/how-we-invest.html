How we invest

A better way to invest super for your future.

The super industry’s purpose is to invest and grow Australia’s retirement savings. But super funds have consistently invested money in fossil fuels and other harmful industries that have set us on a path towards climate change.

Future Super offers a better way to invest super for your future.

Our mission

We grow retirement savings by investing in a future free from climate change and inequality.

What that means

Your money will invest for your financial benefit and for the quality of your future as you save for retirement.

Impact investing

Impact investing is what makes Future Super unique. No other super fund actively seeks investments that provide the greatest opportunity for your money to make a positive impact.

We define impact according to our mission, as meaningful systems change leading to a prosperous future free from climate change and inequality.

An impact investment at Future Super must provide financial value to our members and also make a direct contribution to action on climate change or inequality. Impact investments make up roughly 10% of all investments (by financial value)* made by Future Super.

Choosing impact investments

Choosing impact investments isn’t a cut-and-dry process. Each opportunity is unique and so we focus on the impact objective, or the ability to make a difference. Although that can be anything, there are some categories we keep a close eye on.

Many of Future Super’s impact investments involve the creation of new clean energy, while others create the infrastructure for a more equal future, like the building of disability housing.

The point of these investments is to create tangible systems change that may not happen without our investment, wherever the opportunity may be.

Everything we invest in

There are plenty of regular investments that pass our above screens. Some are companies you’d recognise, like Netflix and Telstra.

We don’t expect every investment to change the world, but we do make all our investments public so that anyone can see where the money goes.

Our voting record

Investing in companies gives Future Super the right to vote on their internal matters and policies. We do this on behalf of our members as a collective, with their values and financial futures in mind. Future Super keeps a transparent record of our voting so you can see how we represent you on issues of climate, equality and corporate governance.
